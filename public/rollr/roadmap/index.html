CRO Roadmap

Three months of sequenced work to move conversion from 3% to 4%, grounded in the findings from our mobile walkthrough audit.

This roadmap turns our 22 audit findings into a sequenced plan of work. Month 1 ships the wizard redesign — the biggest single conversion lever and your top priority. Month 2 tackles the homepage. Month 3 refines based on data and addresses the tail.

Everything traces back to the audit. Every workstream references the specific findings it solves.

3% → 4% Conversion target
57% → 66% Subscription target
3 months Timeframe
Month 1

Foundation + wizard

Quick fixes ship day one. Content and design decisions get locked in the first week. The wizard redesign — your top priority — designs and builds through weeks 2-4.

Day-one fixes
Cart modal is broken on mobile. Error messages persist after resolution. Both cost conversions every day.
Small
#3B1

The checkout button in the cart drawer is hidden behind Chrome's bottom bar on mobile. People who want to buy can't find the button to pay. This is a show-stopper that needs to go live the moment we have Shopify access.

The "Please select a scent" error persists after a scent has been selected. Quick JS fix.

Messaging hierarchy
Define what story Rollr tells cold traffic, in what order. Every build that follows depends on this.
Small
#1#6#15

The information hierarchy across the entire journey is broken. The value proposition isn't clear beyond "it's a beautiful product." Efficacy messaging — the #1 reason customers love Rollr — is buried 3-4 scrolls down. Key FAQ content is hidden behind a sub-sub-menu.

This workstream makes the content decisions: what story, in what order, for cold traffic. Where does efficacy sit? Which objection-handlers get pulled into the purchase flow? These are decisions, not builds — but the wizard and homepage can't be designed without them.

Design system refinement
Lock the visual system before building against it. Draft exists — needs review and sign-off.
Small
#13

The site currently uses irregular shapes, inconsistent fonts, and mixed colour usage. A web design system exists in draft — typography, spacing, colours, components. Needs refinement with you and then locked, so both the wizard and homepage build against the same visual language.

Depends on: messaging hierarchy (content decisions inform visual hierarchy)

Wizard redesign
The entire purchase flow. Pricing clarity, step UX, reviews at decision point, subscription framing — all solved here.
Large
#2#5#7#9#11#12#17#20

This is where you've watched people go in circles. Four prices on one screen, no running total, a subscribe modal that can't be closed, error messages in the brand colour, reviews invisible during the purchase decision, a wizard that doesn't know what's already in the cart.

The redesign preserves the three-step flow that drives 57% subscription uptake while fixing every friction point. Pricing becomes clear. Steps guide rather than confuse. Efficacy and reviews appear where they matter. Subscription framing gets stronger.

Design starts once the messaging hierarchy and design system are locked. Build target: weeks 2-4 of Month 1, with refinements continuing into Month 2 based on data.

Depends on: messaging hierarchy + design system

Remaining quick fixes
Trust signals, header size, site cleanup, press links. Ship throughout the month as they fit.
Small
#10#14#18#22

Add shipping, returns, and guarantee info visible before checkout. Reduce the header size on mobile. Remove "Powered by Shopify" from the footer. Link press quotes to original articles. All independent, all small.

Month 2

Homepage + wizard refinement

The wizard is live and generating data. Refine it based on real behaviour while giving the homepage full attention.

Wizard refinement
First weeks of data show what's working. Adjust pricing framing, step flow, subscription messaging based on real behaviour.
Medium
#2#5#20

The wizard redesign ships in Month 1 but it won't be perfect on day one. Watch the data: where do people drop off? Is pricing clearer? Did subscription uptake hold? Iterate based on evidence, not assumptions.

Depends on: wizard redesign live and generating data

Homepage restructure
75% of traffic lands here from Meta ads. Reorder the story for cold traffic. Resolve the chat widget. Full redesign.
Large
#4#8#15#16

The homepage is a brand experience page doing double duty as a landing page for cold paid traffic. It doesn't work for that job. CTAs reference content that hasn't been shown yet. "How it works" is buried near the bottom. The chat widget competes with Shop Now.

Redesign against the messaging hierarchy locked in Month 1. Reorder sections so cold traffic gets: what it is, how it works, why it's different, then choose yours. Surface key FAQs. Decide on the chat widget (evaluate usage data first). Fix footer labels.

Depends on: messaging hierarchy + design system

Checkout + enhancements
Checkout branding, cross-sell, urgency signals. Only matters once the funnel above is working.
Medium
#19#21B2

Tidy checkout branding (limited by standard Shopify — no Plus). Add cross-sell or gift positioning. Introduce urgency signals using natural scarcity ("each gemstone is hand-carved and naturally unique"), not manufactured countdown timers.

Depends on: wizard + homepage live

Dedicated paid traffic landing page
If cold traffic conversion still lags after the homepage restructure, a stripped-back landing page is the next lever. May extend into a forward engagement.
Large
#4

75% of traffic comes from Meta ads. The homepage restructure in Month 2 will improve things, but the homepage still has to serve both brand-aware and cold visitors. A dedicated landing page for paid traffic can be single-minded: what it is, does it work, how much, buy now. No navigation, no blog links, no brand storytelling — just conversion.

Scope this based on Month 2-3 data. If the restructured homepage is converting cold traffic well, this becomes optional. If there's still a gap, this is the highest-impact next step and a natural foundation for a continued engagement.

Depends on: homepage restructure live + conversion data by traffic source

Measurement + forward roadmap
Document what moved, what didn't, and what to do next.
Small

Compare conversion and subscription rates before and after each phase. Document what worked, what surprised us, what to test next. Build the case for continued engagement or hand off a clear plan.
