Imagery direction

Product: Premium and sensorial. Showcasing textures, embracing mess. The product IS the hero.

Ingredients/Scents: Shot like the product — premium, sensorial. Hands interacting with ingredients. Ice and blue for refreshing. Warm golden light for warmth. Scent-coloured backgrounds to set mood.

People: Authentic, full of life. Dark silhouettes, unusual crops, graphic compositions. Never posed lifestyle shots.

Glass filter: Faceted distortion on imagery — as if looking through the bottle. Sparingly.

Avoid: standard white-background product isolation, stock photography, clinical studio shots.

Conversion principles

How conversion tactics should feel within The Conscious Hedonist identity.

CTA presence

Confident and direct. "Build Your Rollr" over generic "Shop Now." The brand has attitude — buttons should too.

Urgency

Organic over manufactured. "Each gemstone is hand-carved and naturally unique" is authentic scarcity. No countdown timers.

Trust signals

Woven into the product story. Review quotes near the purchase decision. "We do talk about sweat. We do speak transparently."

Subscription framing

"Refill Plan" not "Subscription." Lead with flexibility. Widen the savings gap enough that one-time feels irrational.

Cart and checkout

Drawer cart, no page redirect. Progressive disclosure. Running total visible at all times.

Mobile

Thumb-first. Key actions in bottom half. One decision per screen in the configurator. Smooth as Estrid.

Discussion areas

For discussion

Instrument Sans usage scope

Instrument Sans is used for buttons/CTAs and all functional UI — form inputs, cart line items, meta text, captions. Sans-serif for all interactive elements where readability matters. Confirmed direction.

For discussion

Fluid letterform prominence

Should these be subtle background textures or prominent visual elements in the purchase flow? We recommend prominent in scent selection (step 2 of configurator), subtle elsewhere.

For discussion

Visible grid lines on web

Proposed as subtle accent only — beside hero images, alongside editorial content. One or two per page maximum. Does this feel right, or should it be more present?

For discussion

Subscription savings gap

Conversion evidence suggests widening from 20% to 25-40%. This is a commercial decision, not a design one, but it affects how the subscription comparison UI is designed.
