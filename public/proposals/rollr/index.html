the audit

13 findings, 4 critical

we found 13 issues. here are the ones with the biggest impact on conversion.

1
landing experience vs paid traffic the homepage is a brand experience page, but 75% of visitors arrive from Meta ads. they need to understand what Rollr is and whether it works before the brand story lands
2
configurator asks too much the stepped flow drives 57% subscription uptake — that's worth protecting. but on mobile — where most paid traffic lands — option cards have small tap targets, the information hierarchy is flat, and a carousel hides half the scent options. pricing isn't visible until the final step. this is the likeliest drop-off point for mobile visitors
3
product page doing too much at once the strongest selling points — efficacy, reviews, how it works — all sit below the purchase flow. they should be supporting the decision, not following it
4
efficacy needs to sit higher "it actually works" is the message your customers repeat most. the luxury positioning is strong — efficacy just needs to appear earlier so first-time visitors get the full picture
5
pricing isn't visible early enough total cost is hidden until the final step. "save 20%" doesn't show the pound difference. the subscription maths (£12 every 2 months = £6/month) isn't made visible anywhere
